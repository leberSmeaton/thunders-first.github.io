Wednesday 27th of January 2021

Thoughts on growth and entering the Tech industry

The deep end of Tech, it's pretty deep isn’t it?

And so I jumped, well, more like did a run-up and a GIANT BOMB into one of the deepest pools of knowledge out there. Only a few days ago I 100% decided I was going to be in the tech industry.

Tech Industry is a loaded term though, so much tech, so little time and I felt I was running out of it. I enjoy problem solving and learning new languages but which direction do I go? So far my mentors are telling me it’s okay to not necessarily fix yourself in one direction OR go for gold as a full-stacker.

By chance I've met many senior developers across many facets of the industry, building my network. Their commentary has lifted me up, given me confidence and I thought what is this strange embrace? People actually wanting to see others succeed hadn’t always been a thing in my past work life.

Overwhelmed with starting options is an understatement. Eventually I just picked a couple basic free courses through Codecademy, Mimo and General Assembly. The paid courses I am also attending are SheCodes Plus and General Assembly's Part-Time Web-Development course.

What is my goal? I am to build an online portfolio to show I’m keen, eager, willing, HUNGRY to be a developer! And hopefully with that folio get a scholarship into a bootcamp as too fast track my career. The two bootcamps at Coder Academy and MYOB's DevelopHer also have scholarship opportunities which I'm gunning for.

Even this blog post is ground-breaking for me. I started as writing a goal for future me and now it’s a blog post. I have to say, I'm proud of myself.

Bella Lebsmeaurphy
